# CAATH Enterprise Information Hierarchy

The information hierarchy keeps CAATH dense but readable.

## Attention Priority

1. Workflow execution surface.
2. Live operational indicators.
3. AI and contextual assistance.
4. Governance, collaboration, and knowledge context.
5. Navigation, preferences, and utility controls.

## Executive Scanning Strategy

- Put landing and execution destinations first in navigation.
- Keep metrics quiet and grouped.
- Make only one surface feel primary at a time.
- Use muted supporting panels to preserve context without stealing attention.

## Density Balance

Density is preserved through structured grouping, compact tables, and keyboard-first access. Density should not come from visual compression, repeated borders, or competing badges.
