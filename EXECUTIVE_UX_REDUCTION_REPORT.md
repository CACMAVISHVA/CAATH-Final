# CAATH Executive UX Reduction Report

Date: 2026-06-04

## Objective

Make CAATH feel simple, powerful, fast, executive, and enterprise-grade by reducing visible competition for attention.

## Reductions Completed

| Area | Before | After | Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| Utility rails | Sidebar plus middle rail plus floating launcher | Sidebar plus one right drawer | Less visual competition |
| Alerts/notifications | Bell, rail counters, route-level notification surfaces | One Operations button and drawer | Lower alert fatigue |
| Sidebar | Broad subsystem catalog | Focused workflow map | Faster route selection |
| Pinned/recent nav | Extra sidebar navigation layers | Removed from sidebar | Cleaner scan path |
| AI/collaboration/activity | Separate visible nav concepts | Contextual signals in drawer/search | More executive clarity |

## Current Executive UX Model

The first scan now answers:

- Where do I work? Live Workspace
- Where are my tasks? Task Board
- Where is risk? Operations drawer and Compliance/GST
- Where are approvals? Control
- Where do I search? Header Search

## Remaining Dashboard Reduction Targets

| Target | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Excess metrics | Keep only metrics tied to action: SLA risk, overdue work, notice exposure, approval blockage, revenue risk |
| Duplicate cards | Merge repeated operational health indicators into one executive summary |
| Passive widgets | Remove unless they route to a workflow |
| AI panels | Show only when recommendation confidence and action are available |
| Activity feeds | Use drawer/timeline, not multiple dashboard feeds |

## Enterprise UX Standard

CAATH should feel closer to Linear, Notion, Attio, Stripe Dashboard, and modern SAP Fiori by using:

- restrained navigation
- clear workflow hierarchy
- fewer badges
- predictable action placement
- contextual side surfaces
- high signal density without dashboard clutter

## Success Metric

An executive or operations lead should be able to identify the top operational risk and open the next action in under 10 seconds.
